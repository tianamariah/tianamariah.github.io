📢 2022

  • We would like to formally welcome to the lab a Resident Veterinarian at Penn conducting research in the lab - Imani Nicolis!

  • We would like to formally welcome to the lab our newest Post-Doctoral Researchers: Fungai Musaigwa and Olufemi Akinkuotu!

  • Congrats to our grad student Jay Ortiz for being awarded a 2022 NASEM Ford Foundation Predoctoral Fellowship to study neuroimmune interactions in the sinonasal tract in the context of fungal allergen-mediated Type 2 allergic inflammation. He was also selected as a finalist in the HHMI Gilliam and Blavatnik Family Foundation Fellowship competitions.

  • Congrats to our grad student Jay Ortiz for being selected to give an oral presentation on his project at The American Association of Immunologists (AAI) Annual Meeting in Portland! He will also be presented with the 2022 AAI-Thermo Fisher Trainee Achievement Award, which recognizes up to 6 promising trainees in the field of immunology. Selection is based on career promise and presentation of an outstanding first-author abstract selected for oral presentation in a Block Symposium.

  • Our grad student Jay Ortiz has been elected to the 2022-2023 Graduate and Professional Student Assembly (GAPSA) Executive Board. He is thrilled to lead the IDEAL (Inclusion, Diversity, Equity, Access, and Leadership) Student Council, charged with representing graduate and professional affinity group interests across all of Penn's twelve graduate schools. Jay will also represent GAPSA and IDEAL at the Penn University Council.

  • In his role as IDEAL Council Chair in GAPSA, our grad student Jay Ortiz has led a successful effort to cement the biggest investment in graduate and professional affinity groups in the history of GAPSA to the tune of $150K. These funds will be used to support social, academic, and professional development programming for underrepresented graduate and professional students across all of Penn's twelve graduate schools.

📢 2021

  • Congratulations to our leader De’Broski Herbert for being awarded a Presidential Term Associate Professorship by University of Pennsylvania President President Gutmann. This honor is bestowed among faculty at Penn who contribute to faculty eminence through scholarship and diversity across the University. These honorees are a key feature of Penn’s Action Plan for Faculty Diversity and Excellence and support our School’s diversity, equity, and inclusion priorities. Congrats De’Broski!

  • We have a new publication in PLOS Pathogens! It is titled “Transgenic expression of a T cell epitope in Strongyloides ratti reveals that helminth-specific CD4+ T cells constitute both Th2 and Treg populations.” The work was headed by Bonnie Douglas which was the main work for her thesis. Also contributing to the manuscript was Annabel Ferguson, Chris Pastore, and Li-Yin Hung as well as our collaborators in the Lok lab here at Penn.
